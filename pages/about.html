My experience in tech has been a long one. I graduated with a graphic design degree in 2007. At wich point I went to work in a printing company, but not as a graphic designer on the count that I didn't have the experience. So I did print layout and setup. I worked on the computer and I knew all the programs we were using but didn't have the confidence to express my understanding and knowledge or my willingness to learn a higher position. I felt ostersised most times when I did speak up to my manager to improve upon a process or spoken down to. In 2009 I had my first daughter and became the primary caretaker of my 2 nephews the younger of which was already struggling in and out of group homes. My partner and I was trying to provide stability for the boys and navigate learning to be new parents all at once. After a year I left my job and opted for temp work to have a more flexible schedule while my partner finished his degree and took on a no pay intern-ship. At this point we took custody of my 2 nieces as well and my younger nephew ended up being taken to a juvinile facility. My youngest niece was born with a congenital heart defect and we found that she needed open heart surgery and alot of medical care. After a year my oldest nephew was 16 going on 17 and decided to drop out of high school, against our wishes for him, and take what ever work he could get to try to help with the house hold. It became increasingly harder to maintain a work schedule, doctors appointments and care for her health needs, as well as care for the other children at home at that time. We were very fortunate that my partners internship led to a full time position. I became a stay at home mom and we lived very humbly. My niece has gone on to have 1 more open heart surgery before she went into heart failure and had to be placed on the transplant list early 2013 by the grace of all she recieved her transplant on May 9 2013. It was a 2 year recovery process at which point other health concerns kept coming up till we finally reached a point of stability, we thought. But life doesn't always go how you hope for it to. Early 2015 my niece caught a virus that landed her in the hospital. She had to have 3 blood transfusion wich I provided, thankfully we are the same blood type. I also had a baby girl in April that year. Then she went on to fight this virus with monthly IV infusions for almost 2 years. Constant ups and downs she contracted "A" septic meningitis. My youngest daughter caught pnemonia at 10.5 months that turned out to be MRSA pnemonia. Her left lung callapsed and she has scaring in her lung from it. It took 6 months for her to be clear of the infection and we get told again and again how lucky we are. And through it all or maybe despite it all we tried our best to maintain a stable and happy household. My daughter finally stabalized again and was clear of the virus enough to no longer need IV injections. Only to find out that she had a bout of rejection in 2020 at the same time the pandemic was starting. She also had to have a full spinal fusion in 2021 due to poor muscle mass as a byproduct of all her health problems. We got through it all, and a year later she is clear of rejection with minor cellular damage that will need to be watched but we will take it. She has to have a biopsy yearly to check for anymore rejection or new bouts of rejection but we do what needs to be done. I was out of work for all of it and I feel blessed that I was able to be there for my kids. I now realized that I have to figure out where I am going and what I am going to do. My boys are both in their 20s and my oldest daughter is 18, my second oldest will be 17 late summer, my second to youngest will be 13 mid-summer and my youngest will be 7 in early April. 4 of my children may have started out as my nephews & nieces but through all the hardships, tears and laughter we have a bond that is deep and unbreakable. I decided that I want to be the role model I never had growing up for them, my kids. I want them to know its never to late to start and its never to late to dream. And if you keep dreaming before you know it your dreams have become your life. While coming to this relization my partner came across Tech-Moms and I knew this is where I needed to be. This is where I can take that step forward and start a new part of my journey.
